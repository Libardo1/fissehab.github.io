About Me


I am a fourth year PhD candidate at Hopkins. As a dynamicist, I am interested in understanding drivers of tropical atmospheric dynamics, using observations and modeling, and their impacts around the globe, with the ultimate goal of contributing to our knowledge and management of climate variability and climate change. My main areas of focus are the impacts and prediction of the Madden-Julian Oscillation (MJO) and Equatorial waves, and how they interact with each other and with other modes of various spatiotemporal scales, and how these interactions impact weather and climate in different parts of the world.


My PhD study, which is towards completion, is on how various drivers and their interactions influence rainfall over tropical Africa at interannual and intraseasonal time-scales. I am currently looking for a position that focuses on climate variability and predictability, tropical-extratropical interactions, air-sea interaction, coupling between tropical convection and large scale atmospheric circulation, and large-scale atmospheric dynamics.
