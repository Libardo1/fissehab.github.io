I am a fifth year PhD candidate at Johns Hopkins University, department of Earth and Planetary Sciences. My PhD study is in atmospheric physics and dynamics. I completed my undergraduate studies in civil engineering, master’s degree in hydro-climatology and a second master’s degree in atmospheric physics. For the last six plus years, I have been analysing large volume climate and hydrology data to understand physical processes, to develop statistical rainfall prediction models and to evaluate how climate change may impact large river basins such as the Nile.
